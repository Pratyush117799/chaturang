Vyuha Niti (Battle Formations)
Dhanurveda, the ancient Indian science of archery and warfare, describes complex geometric military arrays called Vyuhas. These were used to maximize defensive strength or crack open enemy lines. Our Interactive Vyuha Visualizer maps these ancient blueprints onto the Chaturanga framework.
Formation Name Meaning / Shape Primary Tactical Purpose Mahabharata Context
Chakra Vyuha Wheel or Disc Impenetrable, rotating concentric defense Used by Drona to trap Abhimanyu on the 13th day of the Kurukshetra war.
Padma Vyuha Blooming Lotus Surround and envelop Often utilized alongside Chakra Vyuha to crush penetrating forces.
Garuda Vyuha Eagle Aggressive piercing wedge Favored by Bhishma to break through dense infantry lines.
Makara Vyuha Crocodile / Sea Monster Pincer attack using strong flanks Deployed by the Kauravas to counter half-moon formations.
Shakata Vyuha Cart or Box Mobile, defensible escort Used to protect high-value targets while moving lines.
