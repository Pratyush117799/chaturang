Pāśa — The Dice Game
One Loaded Die
पाशा · The game within the game
"The Kauravas had a die that could not lose. Shakuni threw it for Duryodhana at the Hastinapura court. Every roll was predetermined. The greatest war in history began not with swords — but with a loaded die."
— Sabha Parva, Mahabharata
⚄ One player secretly holds the Pāśa — a loaded die they can invoke once, at any moment.
🎯 When invoked, they choose the dice result — any piece type, any face. No one can stop it.
💥 The moment they use it, every other player is told. The betrayal is revealed. Their dharma collapses.
⚖ They may have just won the game. Or given their enemies the fury of a righteous cause. That is the Pāśa.
🙈 The Pāśa holder is assigned secretly — even they don't know until they check privately. Pass the device around.
